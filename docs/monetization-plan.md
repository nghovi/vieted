# Monetization Plan

## Goal

Build a monetization model that matches VietEd's product promise: structured AI learning for Vietnamese students, with parents paying for better outcomes and clearer progress.

The first revenue model should optimize for:

- Fast MVP launch
- Clear value to parents
- Low product complexity
- Measurable conversion points

## Recommendation

Start with a freemium subscription model aimed at parents of students in grades 6-9.

Why this is the best first fit:

- Parents already pay for tutoring and test-prep support
- Subscription revenue matches repeated study behavior
- The product already plans premium-feeling features such as review plans, progress tracking, and parent summaries
- Ads would weaken trust and distract from learning
- School contracts are attractive later, but they add longer sales cycles and operational complexity

## Monetization Principles

- Do not monetize through ads in the MVP
- Charge for outcomes, consistency, and visibility, not for basic access
- Keep the free tier useful enough to demonstrate value
- Put paywalls after meaningful product value is shown
- Make parent-facing benefits as clear as student-facing benefits

## Best First Revenue Model

### 1. Free tier

Use the free tier to prove the learning loop works.

Suggested limits:

- 1 to 2 guided lessons per day
- Limited AI follow-up questions
- Limited quiz generation
- Basic streaks and topic progress
- Short review queue with shallow history

Free users should still be able to experience:

- Topic learning
- Quiz feedback
- Some personalized review

The goal of free is activation and habit formation, not full replacement of paid tutoring.

### 2. Plus subscription

This should be the first paid plan.

Suggested benefits:

- Unlimited daily tutoring sessions
- More quiz sets and mock tests
- Full mistake history and review recommendations
- Parent-friendly weekly progress summary
- Deeper mastery tracking by topic
- Priority access to new subjects or exam-prep packs

This plan should be positioned as affordable support between school and private tutoring.

### 3. Later plans

After product-market fit is clearer, add:

- Family plan for multiple children
- Exam-prep packs for specific grades or subjects
- Tutoring-center dashboard with student seats
- School or center licensing for assignments and reporting

These are strong later expansions, but they should not block the first monetized launch.

## Pricing Direction

Do not finalize pricing before speaking with parents, but start with a simple range hypothesis.

Suggested early pricing hypothesis:

- Monthly plan: priced below one tutoring session
- Quarterly plan: 15 to 20 percent discount versus monthly
- Trial: 7 days or a limited number of premium study sessions

For Vietnam, the positioning should feel clearly cheaper than private tutoring while still premium enough to signal quality.

The pricing message should be:

"Daily AI-supported learning and review for less than the cost of occasional tutoring."

## What To Charge For

Strong candidates for premium gating:

- Unlimited AI tutor messages
- Personalized daily review queue
- Full progress history
- Parent summaries
- Full mock tests and exam-style practice packs
- Advanced mistake analysis

Weak candidates for premium gating:

- Initial onboarding
- First lesson experience
- First quiz on a topic
- Basic topic browsing

If users hit a paywall before they see personalized value, conversion will likely be weak.

## Suggested Paywall Moments

The best early paywall moments are after value is obvious.

Recommended order to test:

1. After a student completes a lesson and quiz, show that a personalized review plan is ready for upgrade.
2. After the student uses the daily free AI quota, offer unlimited help through Plus.
3. After a parent opens a partial weekly summary, gate full reporting and recommendations behind Plus.

Avoid showing a paywall before the student has:

- Completed at least one meaningful study session
- Seen mistake explanations
- Understood that progress is being tracked

## Funnel Design

Track the funnel from first visit to payment.

Suggested funnel:

1. Landing page visit
2. Signup
3. Grade and subject selection
4. First lesson started
5. First quiz completed
6. First review recommendation shown
7. Paywall viewed
8. Trial started
9. Subscription activated
10. Week-2 retained paid user

This is the minimum funnel needed to understand whether monetization is working.

## Parent Positioning

The paying customer will often be the parent, even if the daily user is the student.

Parent value proposition:

- Less wasted study time
- Better understanding of weak topics
- Structured review without constant supervision
- Lower cost than repeated tutoring
- Visible progress over time

Student value proposition:

- Easier explanations in Vietnamese
- Fast feedback on mistakes
- Less boring review
- Clear next steps each day

The product should speak to both, but payment UX should likely be parent-oriented.

## MVP Rollout Plan

### Phase 1: Validate demand

- Interview 10 to 15 parents of grades 6-9 students
- Test reactions to subscription versus one-time payment
- Identify strongest value hooks: unlimited tutor, review plan, or progress reporting
- Confirm acceptable monthly price ranges

### Phase 2: Ship monetization-ready product hooks

- Add usage limits for free users
- Add entitlement checks in backend and UI
- Add paywall screens tied to natural moments
- Add trial support
- Add basic billing events and analytics

### Phase 3: Optimize conversion

- A/B test where the paywall appears
- Compare message framing for parents versus students
- Test monthly versus quarterly default
- Measure which premium feature drives the highest conversion

## Metrics To Watch

Monetization metrics:

- Free-to-paid conversion rate
- Trial-to-paid conversion rate
- Paid retention at 30 and 90 days
- Average revenue per paying family
- Churn reasons

Product metrics connected to revenue:

- First lesson completion
- First quiz completion
- Review return rate
- Weekly active students
- Parent summary open rate
- Student streak length

If retention is weak, improving monetization mechanics alone will not solve the business.

## Risks

### Risk 1: Charging too early

If the paywall appears before users trust the product, conversions will be low.

### Risk 2: Overbuilding plans too soon

Multiple plan types, school sales, and family billing can add complexity before the core student loop is proven.

### Risk 3: Weak parent-facing value

If the app feels like generic AI chat, parents may not see why it deserves a subscription.

### Risk 4: Cost-heavy usage

Unlimited AI usage can create margin pressure. Set sensible fair-use policies and monitor usage by plan.

## Sharp Recommendation

The first monetization move for VietEd should be:

- Freemium product
- Parent-paid Plus subscription
- Paywall after a completed lesson and quiz
- Premium value centered on unlimited tutoring, review plans, and parent progress summaries

Do not start with ads, school contracts, or complex classroom systems. First prove that students return, parents see progress, and a simple subscription can convert.

1. Monetization system
   Define the first paid offering as a parent-paid subscription for students, not ads.
   Launch with freemium access: free daily study quota, paid unlimited tutoring, quizzes, and review history.
   Add subscription entitlements to the product model: free, plus, and later center plans.
   Instrument the funnel: visitor -> signup -> first lesson -> first quiz -> paywall hit -> trial -> paid.
   Decide where the first paywall appears: after daily AI limit, after review history depth, or before parent reports.
   Validate willingness to pay with 10 to 15 parent interviews before finalizing pricing.

2. School-like system
   Explore cohort or classroom features only after the solo student loop is sticky.
   Start small with structured study sessions, timed practice, streaks, and weekly goals before virtual AI classes.
   Treat 15-minute quizzes, mid-term style tests, and final review packs as assessment formats, not full school simulation.
   Add gamification carefully: streaks, mastery badges, and weekly targets should support learning rather than distract from it.

3. Student feedback system
   Add lightweight feedback prompts after lessons, quizzes, and review sessions.
   Capture both rating and open text so we can separate content quality issues from UX issues.
   Tag feedback by grade, subject, topic, and session type for easier prioritization.
   Review feedback weekly and connect it to retention, quiz completion, and repeat study sessions.
